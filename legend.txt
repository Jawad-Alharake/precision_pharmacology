Legend & Glossary of Terms

1. Biological Institutions (Research Origins)

    BRD: Broad Institute Research Drug (An internal identification code used for proprietary compounds developed at the Broad Institute in Boston).

    KU: University of Kansas.

    VU: Vanderbilt University (Nashville).

2. Experimental Design & Treatment

    mcf7: Michigan Cancer Foundation-7 (A standard metastatic breast cancer cell line).

    MoA: Mechanism of Action (The biological "how" of a drug; how it interacts with the body).

    pert: Perturbagen (Any substance or genetic modification used to disturb a cell).

    trt_cp: Treatment Compound (A physical drug or small molecule).

    trt_oe: Treatment Overexpression (Genetic engineering to artificially increase a gene's activity).

    trt_sh: Treatment shRNA (Genetic engineering to artificially "silence" or turn off a gene's activity).

3. Genomic & Platform Definitions

    BING: Best Inferred Genes (A subset of ~9,000 computationally predicted genes that have a very high confidence score).

    INF: Inferred Genes (Genes that are not physically measured, but mathematically predicted).

    LINCS L1000: Library of Integrated Network-based Cellular Signatures Landmark 1000.

    LM: Landmark Genes (The 978 core genes physically measured by the assay).

    PR: Platform Resource (A standardized platform identifier).

4. Data Structure & Metadata

    cids: Column IDs (Identifies the specific drug experiments or "signatures").

    distil_id: Distilled Experiment (A unique ID assigned to an individual laboratory sample).

    iname: Investigative Name (The laboratory or research name for a drug).

    rids: Row IDs (Identifies the specific genes).

    sig: Signature (The unique genetic "fingerprint" produced by an experiment).

5. File Formats & Technical Identifiers

    CID: Compound Identifier (The official, unique public database number assigned to a molecule by the NCBI PubChem database).

    GCT: Gene Expression Dataset (A human-readable text format for matrix data).

    GCTX: Gene Expression Dataset (A binary, high-performance format designed for massive datasets).

    LXB: Luminex Binary (The raw data coming directly from the laboratory optical sensors).

    MODZ: Moderated Z-score (A standardized score averaged across multiple replicate experiments to ensure accuracy).

    QC: Quality Control.

    SMILES: Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System (A notation for describing the physical 3D structure of chemical molecules using short text strings).
